Damodar River

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·      <!--\[endif]-->Meaning:

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->o   <!--\[endif]-->Means "**rope around the belly**", derived from Sanskrit (dama) "rope" and (udara) "belly". This is another name of the Hindu god Krishna, given to him because his foster-mother tied him to a large urn.

 

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·      <!--\[endif]-->Source:  

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->o   <!--\[endif]-->rises in the Palamau hills of Chota Nagpur at an elevation of about 609.75 m        

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·      <!--\[endif]-->Length:    

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->o   <!--\[endif]-->Nearly 547 kms

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·      <!--\[endif]-->Tributary:

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->o   <!--\[endif]-->Konar, Barakar, Haharo, Bokaro, Ghari, Jamunia, Khadia, Guaia and Bhera

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·      <!--\[endif]-->Dams:      

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->o   <!--\[endif]-->The **Damodar Valley Corporation**, which began work in 1948, constructed four multipurpose dams to form a series of reservoirs—Tilaiya, Maithon, Konar, and Panchet Hill.

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->ü  <!--\[endif]-->The first dam was built across the Barakar River, a tributary of the Damodar river at Tilaiya in 1953.

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->ü  <!--\[endif]-->The second one was built across the Konar River, another tributary of the Damodar river at Konar in 1955.

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->ü  <!--\[endif]-->Two dams across the rivers Barakar and Damodar were built at Maithon in 1957 and Panchet in 1958. Both the dams are some 8 kms upstream of the confluence point of the rivers.

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->o   <!--\[endif]-->**Durgapur Barrage** was constructed downstream of the four dams in 1955, across the Damodar river at Durgapur, with head regulators for canals on either side for feeding an extensive system of canals and distributaries.

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->o   <!--\[endif]-->In 1978, the government of Bihar constructed the Tenughat Dam across the Damodar river outside the control of DVC.

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->ü  <!--\[endif]-->Constructing a dam across the Barakar river at Belpahari in Jharkhand state is also proposed.

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·      <!--\[endif]-->Industries:

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->o   <!--\[endif]-->The Damodar valley is called “the Ruhr of India”.

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->o   <!--\[endif]-->The Damodar valley along the Bihar–West Bengal border includes India’s most important coal- and mica-mining fields and has long been an area of active industrial development. The major coalfields (Jharia, Raniganj, and Giridih) are mostly open-pit and are easily mined.

 

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·      <!--\[endif]-->City:         

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·      <!--\[endif]-->Flow: 

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->o   <!--\[endif]-->It flows in a south-easterly direction entering the deltaic plains below Raniganj.

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->o   <!--\[endif]-->Near Burdwan, the river abruptly changes its course to a southerly direction and joins Hooghly about 48.27 km below Calcutta.

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->o   <!--\[endif]-->The Damodar Valley is spread across Hazaribagh, Ramgarh, Koderma, Giridih, Dhanbad, Bokaro and Chatra districts in Jharkhand and Bardhaman and Hooghly districts in West Bengal and partially covers Palamu, Ranchi, Lohardaga and Dumka districts in Jharkhand and Howrah, Bankura and Purulia districts in West Bengal with a command area of 24,235 square kms.
